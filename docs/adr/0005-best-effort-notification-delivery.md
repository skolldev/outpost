# Best-effort notification delivery

Outpost delivers Notifications on a recorded best-effort basis: each Notification is persisted as a row in PostgreSQL and sent asynchronously with a few in-process retries, but there is no durable delivery queue and no redelivery across restarts. A Notification that fails after its retries, exceeds the per-channel rate cap, or is lost to a shutdown mid-send is recorded (or left stale) rather than retried later. This matches the stance of best-effort ingestion and the PostgreSQL-only dependency policy: a missed chat message is recoverable because the underlying Issue or Incident remains visible in Outpost, whereas guaranteed at-least-once delivery would require a durable outbox with recovery sweeps, idempotency handling, and duplicate suppression whose main protected failure mode (a crash during the seconds of a send) is far rarer than the failure modes visibility already solves (dead webhook URLs, receiver outages).
